Direct Free Kicks

A ‘free kick’ is exactly how it sounds. A player gets to kick the ball without any players opposing them. A direct kick means that a player can take a direct shot at the goal and score.

Awarded For

  • A referee gives direct kicks if a player is careless, reckless, or uses excessive force when committing fouls like striking, tackling, or tripping an opponent.

Referee Signals

  • Whistle blown
  • Empty hand points to the attacking team (no angle)
  • Pointed team receives free kick

What happens

  • A free-kick in soccer restarts the game after a team commits a foul. A foul is an offense and is against the soccer laws.
  • The opposition stands 10 yards away from the ball for the kick to be taken
