Corner Kick

A 'Corner kick' is the method of restarting play when the ball goes out of play over the goal line. The kick is taken from the corner of the field of play nearest to the place where the ball crossed the goal line.


Awarded For
A corner kick is awarded when the whole of the ball passes over the goal line, on the ground or in the air, having last touched a player of the defending team, and a goal is not scored.
Referee Signals
By default, no whistle is required for a corner kick.
The referee points at the corner flag with his arm pointed upward.
What happens
The ball must be stationary and on the ground within the corner area near the flagpost.
All opposing players must be at least 10 yards from the corner area until the ball is in play.
