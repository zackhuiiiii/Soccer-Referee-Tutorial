Indirect Free Kicks

'Free kick' is exactly how it sounds. A player gets to kick the ball without any players opposing them. An indirect kick means that another player must touch the ball before a goal can be scored.


Awarded For
  • Offside
  • Illegal handling by the goalkeeper within the penalty area
  • Preventing the goalkeeper from releasing the ball from the hands
  • Errors by the goalkeeper like holding the ball for more than 6 seconds
Referee Signals
Whistle blown
Empty hand points to the sky
Pointed team receives indirect free kick
What happens
