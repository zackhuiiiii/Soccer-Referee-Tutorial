Advantage

An 'advantage' is given when a foul and stoppage in play would actually be harmful to the offended team, as determined by the discretion of the official.

Awarded For
The official is permitted to disregard the foul and let the game continue. Itâ€™s usually given when a team that has been fouled retains possession in the attacking third of the field.
Referee Signals
Referee does not blow the whistle for this signal.
Both arms put out parallelly
Pointing to the goal of the team that has the advantage.
What happens
The game proceeds normally if the team that has been fouled retains possession.
If the advantage does not materialize and the team that fouled obtains possession immediately, the referee blows the whistle and awards a free kick.
